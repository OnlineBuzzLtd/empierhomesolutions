# CRM User Manual

This guide is written for everyday users of the CRM.

Use it if you are an owner, office manager, sales person, accounts person, or engineer and you simply want to know:

- where to log in
- what each area does
- how to create and manage records
- what to do if something looks wrong

## 1. What This CRM Does

This CRM is the main system for running the business.

You can use it to:

- manage leads and customers
- create and schedule jobs
- assign engineers
- create quotes and invoices
- track payments
- manage compliance records
- store job documents
- run the CRM for more than one business workspace

## 2. Website Links

Live CRM:

- `https://empire-home-solutions.vercel.app`

Login page:

- `https://empire-home-solutions.vercel.app/login`

New workspace signup:

- `https://empire-home-solutions.vercel.app/signup`

## 3. Before You Start

You will need:

- your email address
- your password
- the correct workspace if you belong to more than one business

If you do not have a login, ask the business owner or CRM admin.

## 4. Logging In

1. Open the login page.
2. Enter your email address.
3. Enter your password.
4. Click `Sign In`.

If your account belongs to more than one workspace, make sure you are in the right workspace after login.

## 5. Main Areas of the CRM

After logging in, you will see a menu.

The main sections are:

- `Dashboard`
- `Leads`
- `Customers`
- `Jobs`
- `Calendar`
- `Quotes`
- `Invoices`
- `Staff`
- `Reports`
- `Settings`

Engineers will usually see fewer sections than office staff or admins.

## 6. Dashboard

The Dashboard gives you a quick view of what is happening today.

You will usually see:

- open jobs
- jobs due today
- unpaid invoices
- open leads

Use the dashboard when you first log in each day.

## 7. Leads

Use `Leads` for new enquiries that are not yet active jobs.

Typical lead stages:

- new
- contacted
- follow up
- survey booked
- quoted
- accepted
- booked
- lost

### To add a lead

1. Open `Leads`.
2. Click the add or create lead option.
3. Fill in the customer name and contact details.
4. Add the service and job type if known.
5. Set the current lead status.
6. Save.

### Best practice

- Keep lead notes short and useful.
- Always set the next action date if the lead is still active.
- Move the lead stage forward as soon as something changes.

## 8. Customers

Use `Customers` for people or businesses you already work with.

A customer record stores:

- name
- phone
- email
- address
- notes
- linked jobs
- linked sites

### To add a customer

1. Open `Customers`.
2. Click `Add Customer`.
3. Fill in the basic contact details.
4. Save.

### Site records

Some customers have a different job address from their billing address.

That is why the CRM supports `Sites`.

A site can include:

- site name or label
- site address
- site contact
- access notes
- parking notes

Use sites when:

- a landlord owns more than one property
- a customer lives at one address but work happens at another
- a tenant or caretaker is the on-site contact

## 9. Jobs

Use `Jobs` to manage actual work being delivered.

A job can include:

- customer
- site
- site contact
- service
- job type
- scheduled date
- assigned engineers
- notes
- job phases
- variations
- documents

### To create a job

1. Open `Jobs`.
2. Click `Add Job`.
3. Select the customer.
4. Add the job title.
5. Choose the site and site contact if needed.
6. Choose the service and job type.
7. Set the date.
8. Assign one or more engineers.
9. Add the description or special notes.
10. Save.

### Common job statuses

- enquiry
- booked
- in progress
- completed
- invoiced

### Good job record habits

- always assign the right site
- always add engineer notes if the team needs special access instructions
- keep job status up to date
- upload photos and documents to the job instead of keeping them in WhatsApp or email

## 10. Calendar

Use `Calendar` to see booked work and upcoming activity.

This includes:

- calls
- follow ups
- surveys
- engineer bookings
- reminders

### Use the calendar to

- check what is happening today
- see who is booked where
- avoid double-booking
- move work to a different day if needed

## 11. Quotes

Use `Quotes` to send pricing to a customer.

The CRM supports:

- quotes
- estimates
- versions
- acceptance tracking

### To create a quote

1. Open `Quotes`.
2. Click `Create Quote`.
3. Link it to the correct customer and job.
4. Add the line items.
5. Check VAT and totals.
6. Save the draft.
7. Publish or send it when ready.

### Quote versions

If the customer asks for changes:

- create a new version instead of overwriting the old one

This helps you keep a clean history of what was priced and when.

### Acceptance

Once the customer agrees:

- mark the quote as accepted
- make sure the team is working from the accepted version

## 12. Invoices

Use `Invoices` to bill the customer.

The CRM supports:

- deposit invoices
- stage invoices
- final invoices
- payment tracking

### To create an invoice

1. Open `Invoices` or create it from the job/quote.
2. Choose the customer and linked job.
3. Add the invoice items or use the staged billing flow.
4. Check totals.
5. Save and send.

### Payment status

Keep invoice status up to date, for example:

- unpaid
- paid
- overdue
- void

## 13. Compliance and Safety

The CRM supports compliance records on jobs.

These can include:

- hazards
- checklists
- certificates
- compliance documents

### Why this matters

Jobs should not be treated as fully complete if required compliance items are still missing.

### Typical use

Before closing a job, make sure:

- hazards are recorded or marked hazard-free
- required checklist items are done
- required certificates are uploaded or completed

## 14. Purchase Orders and Supplier Costs

Use the job costing areas to track:

- purchase orders
- supplier invoices
- supplier credits
- reconciliation against the job

This helps you understand whether a job is actually profitable.

## 15. Staff

Use `Staff` to manage team members.

You may be able to store:

- name
- role
- contact details
- agreed hours
- pay notes
- certifications

Only admins and management users should normally edit staff records.

## 16. Reports

Use `Reports` to check how the business is performing.

Typical report areas:

- revenue
- unpaid invoices
- jobs completed
- lead conversion
- engineer workload

Use reports for weekly reviews, month-end checks, and chasing unpaid work.

## 17. Settings

Use `Settings` to manage the workspace itself.

This may include:

- business name
- branding
- phone number
- email address
- VAT number
- Gas Safe details
- services
- job types
- suppliers
- products
- quote templates
- user roles

Only authorised users should change settings.

## 18. Workspaces and Multi-Tenant Use

This CRM can run more than one business workspace.

That means:

- each business has its own data
- users only see the workspaces they belong to
- records from one workspace should not appear in another

If you can access more than one workspace:

1. Look for the workspace switcher near the top of the CRM.
2. Select the workspace you want.
3. Wait for the CRM to reload.
4. Check that you are viewing the right business before editing anything.

## 19. Demo Mode

Some accounts are demo-only accounts.

If you see labels like:

- `Demo Account`
- `Demo Mode`
- `Demo account: you are viewing demo CRM records only`

then you are not in the live business workspace.

### Important

If you are logged in with a demo account, you cannot fully leave demo mode by pressing `Exit Demo`.

You must:

1. sign out
2. sign back in using a real non-demo user account

## 20. Simple Daily Workflow

For office staff, the normal flow is:

1. Check `Dashboard`
2. Review `Leads`
3. Create or update `Customers`
4. Create or update `Jobs`
5. Use `Calendar` to schedule the work
6. Create `Quotes`
7. Mark accepted quotes
8. Raise `Invoices`
9. Track payments

For engineers, the normal flow is:

1. Log in
2. Check assigned jobs
3. Open today’s job
4. Read site notes carefully
5. Update notes/photos/status
6. Complete compliance items
7. Mark the job complete when finished

## 21. Common Problems

### I cannot log in

Check:

- your email address is correct
- your password is correct
- you are using the live login URL

If needed, ask the admin to reset your password.

### I can log in but cannot see the right data

Check:

- you are in the correct workspace
- you are not using a demo account
- your user role is correct

### I cannot edit something

Possible reasons:

- your role does not allow it
- you are in demo mode
- the record belongs to another workspace

### I cannot see a customer, quote, or job

Check:

- you are in the right workspace
- the record has actually been saved
- filters are not hiding it

## 22. Admin Checklist

If you are the business owner or CRM admin, check these regularly:

- staff have the correct roles
- the workspace branding and company details are correct
- services and job types are up to date
- suppliers and products are current
- quotes and invoices use the right numbering
- engineers have active certifications where needed

## 23. Quick Support Notes

When reporting a problem, include:

- your email address
- your workspace name
- the page you were on
- what you clicked
- what you expected to happen
- what actually happened
- a screenshot if possible

This makes problems much faster to fix.

## 24. Final Advice

Use the CRM as the single source of truth.

That means:

- keep customer details updated
- keep job notes in the job
- store documents in the CRM
- update statuses as work moves forward
- avoid tracking live work in scattered texts, emails, and handwritten notes

If the team uses the CRM properly every day, quotes, jobs, invoicing, and reporting become much easier to manage.
