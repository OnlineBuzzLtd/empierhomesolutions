# Empire CRM Demo Guide for Trade Buyers

## Purpose of This Guide

This document is designed to sit alongside the live CRM demo.

It follows the exact order of the demo walkthrough so a trades business owner, manager, or operations lead can understand what each step is proving and why it matters commercially.

This is not a technical specification. It is a buyer-facing explanation of the value of the system.

## What the Demo Is Showing

The demo is built to show that Empire CRM is not just a contact list or job board. It is an end-to-end operating system for a trade business.

The key idea is simple:

- leads come in
- leads become customers
- customers become booked jobs
- jobs produce quotes, invoices, notes, photos, payments, and documents
- management gets reporting from the same records the team uses day to day

For a plumbing, heating, boiler, or multi-service business, that means less process drift and less information spread across separate tools.

## Step 1. Dashboard

### What the buyer is seeing

The dashboard gives management a fast overview of what is happening in the business right now.

It surfaces:

- open jobs
- today’s installs
- unpaid invoices
- open leads
- recent customer activity

### What to say

This is the morning control panel for the business. Instead of checking five different places, the owner or office manager can see workload, cash flow pressure, and urgent activity from one screen.

### Why it matters to a trades business

- helps the office know what is booked today
- shows whether unpaid money is building up
- keeps the pipeline visible
- gives immediate operational awareness before opening individual records

## Engineer Workflow Talking Point

Inside the job flow, engineers get a simplified field-first experience rather than a cluttered admin dashboard.

That includes:

- next job visibility
- customer contact details
- directions
- quick status actions
- notes and photos near the top of the screen

This is important because a plumber or heating engineer does not want to fight the software while standing outside a property.

## AI Talking Point During the Jobs Step

This is the best place in the demo to show the AI value.

Open the demo job detail page and show the `AI Assist` panel.

### What AI Assist does

It gives the engineer short, practical draft help based on the job record.

Current actions include:

- `Summarize Job`
- `Draft Arrival Note`
- `Draft Work Completed Note`
- `Draft Customer Update`
- `Check Missing Evidence`

### What to say

This is not AI for the sake of it. It is there to reduce admin load on engineers and improve note quality. The engineer can get a quick job summary, draft a clean arrival note, draft a completion note, or check whether photos and documents are still missing.

### Why this matters commercially

- less time spent typing notes
- better consistency in records
- fewer missed photos or missing documents
- easier handover between office and field staff
- better customer communication

### Important trust point

The AI does not silently save into the CRM.

The flow is:

- AI drafts
- the engineer reviews
- the engineer decides whether to use it
- the normal note workflow is still used

That keeps staff in control and makes the feature more practical for real businesses.

## Where the AI Fits in the Overall Sale

The AI story should support the product, not distract from it.

The strongest positioning is:

- the CRM already handles the full operational flow
- AI improves the speed and quality of the field workflow
- management can also explore AI as an add-on capability through the AI Hub

For most trade buyers, the most persuasive AI feature is not a flashy chatbot. It is the fact that engineers can produce better notes, better updates, and better compliance follow-through with less admin effort.
